About Me

This is Dhamar.

When she was 7, she’d look up at the stars and worry that someday, they’d get tired, fizz out, and crash to Earth.

Like any curious child, she asked her dad: “How can the stars stay up like that?”

Like any nonchalant adult, he replied: “That’s just what stars do.” Dhamar wasn’t satisfied.

Dhamar had two dangerous weapons for any 7-year-old: questions, and a library card.

From the bottom shelf of her local library’s encyclopedia section, she pulled out the volume titled ‘S’ and began her star search.

Stars have inward and outward pulls, in which the countering gravitational forces allow them to stay up in the sky! Massive stars become supernovas at the end of their lives and don’t crash onto Earth!

And this pretty much sums up who Dhamar is.
She likes to ask questions and find their riveting answers.
