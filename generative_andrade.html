Generating Architecture as Space Rooms

  • p5.js
  • Computational Design
  • Generative Art

This design generator takes inspiration from Harvard's Memorial Hall architecture and transforms them into optical "rooms" based on Edna Andrade's artwork.


This was created as a final project for my course ES138: Computation Design Values, an engineering science course taught with the Harvard Graduate School of Design.

Project Inspiration & Vision

This project was inspired by Op Artist Edna Andrade’s artwork, which I aimed to recreate using her shape rules and system while applying a separate source of inspiration. Given that Andrade’s work resembles interior rooms with windows, I decided to turn this perspective outward and use the facade of Harvard’s Memorial Hall as inspiration for the interior designs.

Analyzing Andrade's Geometric System

In order to understand and recreate Andrade's style, I analyzed the rules used in her artworks, evaluating how she determined how to divide areas of her canvas into calculated sections and where to place certain shapes.

While seemingly random at a viewer's first glance, Andrade’s paintings operate through carefully organized geometric systems with principles of modularity.

For example, we see that her artpiece Space Dream is divided into 6 major modular regions along the edges of the canvas.

Analyzing Architecture

What architectural features are preserved after transforming a building’s exterior design via a computational grammar and how does this affect the recognizability of the building?

What elements are truly important to a building’s facade?

Given that Memorial Hall is an example of Gothic architecture, I spent some time analyzing historical architectural features of Gothic buildings to understand the design principles that shape their structures.

Memorial Hall, like many European Gothic buildings, is characterized by its pointed arches, ribbed vaults, lancets, and rose windows. These elements are distributed across the facade using rules of repetition, symmetry, and division of levels.

Creating the Program

The implementation of my project was primarily based on creating a p5.js program that would generate Andrade-style designs based on the extracted features. The program featured 3 levels of abstraction: high, medium, and low.

High Abstraction Medium Abstraction Low Abstraction
More heavily influenced by Andrade’s style and shape rules Continues to maintain Andrade-style tiles Less influence from Andrade’s geometric rules and constraints
Only colors from Memorial Hall are used Implements constraints on shape and element placement; embeds more architectural features from Memorial Hall (arcs and lancet openings) Embeds more features from Memorial Hall (arched opening, rose window, tracery, banners)
